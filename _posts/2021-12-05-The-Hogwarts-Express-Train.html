The Hogwarts Express is a real train, a fictional magic train that appears in the "Harry Potter" series of novels [1].

This train runs between London and Hogwarts. In "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" [2], Harry met his two best friends Ron and Hermione during the first ride. The train's departure station is Platform 9Â¾ at King's Cross Station in London [3].

Hogsmeade Village Railway Station was filmed in the picturesque village of Gosland, Yorkshire, but when you see the Harry Potter train crossing the railway bridge to Hogwarts in the movie, you are actually on it What you saw was the world-famous Jacoby steam train passing the magnificent 21-arched Glenfinnan Viaduct in the Scottish Highlands. Built in 1901, this iconic railway viaduct in Scotland is 30 meters high and 381 meters long. It is also the oldest concrete railway viaduct in the world.

In real life, the train's route is described as the world's greatest rail journey, with beautiful scenery, passing through many beautiful Harry Potter shooting locations in Scotland.
